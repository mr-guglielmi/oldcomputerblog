The Atari STacy2
An Attempted Redefinition of the Portable Computer

By: Lilah Beck


Introduction

The Atari STacy2 was the second of four Atari STacy models. It was released and manufactured by the Atari Corporation (United States) in the fall of September 1989. At the date of its release the STacy2 sold for approximately $3173.05 CAD, which would be around $8046.50 CAD today. The computer was designed to be a portable version of the Atari ST computer.


Failed Attempt at Portability

The Atari STacy2 was originally designed to be a smaller, lighter, and more portable version of the Atari ST, but there were some major design flaws that prevented this. The computer weighed in at a whopping 7Kg or 15.5 pounds! At first it was designed to be able to run off of twelve C cell flashlight batteries, but the STacy2 used up the battery power too quickly. The Atari corporation thought that it would just be best to glue the battery compartment shut and plug the computer into the wall for power. The idea of a portable ST computer was great, but the execution was poor.


Computer Specifications & Features

The Atari STacy2 used a Motorola 68HC000, running at 8MHz. Compared to the first model of the STacy that only has 1 MB (megabyte) of RAM (Random Access Memory), the STacy2 has 2MB of RAM. The computer comes with a 3.5” disk that serves as a method of storage, and a 20MB hard drive. The Atari STacy2 also features a trackball and a monochrome 640 x 640 pixel screen. It has five different ports, 1 parallel port, 1 serial port, 1 FDD port, and 2 MIDI ports.








Computer Software & Programming Languages

Much like the Atari ST, and all of the other models of the Atari STacy, the STacy2 uses the Atari TOS 1.04 (The Operating System) software. TOS is able to run programs such as desktop, GEM (Graphics Environment Manager), GEMDOS (GEM Disk Operating System), BIOS (Basic Input/Output System), and XBIOS (Extended BIOS). The STacy2 supports multiple different programming languages, some notable ones are BASIC, C, and PASCAL.


Uses

The Atari STacy2 was most used to run music sequencing software and controlling MIDI instruments by musicians. Because the computer had 2 built- in MIDI ports and was portable (for the most part) It served as a great computer option for musicians. The STacy2 was also used as a general purpose portable computer for the Atari TOS software, word processing, database, and spreadsheets in a business or home office environment.
